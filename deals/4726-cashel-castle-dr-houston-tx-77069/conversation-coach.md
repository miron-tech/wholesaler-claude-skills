## REO Conversation Strategy

### Who You're Talking To
- Listing agent representing the trust/servicer
- Agent doesn't have emotional attachment
- Agent gets commission — they want the deal to close
- But agent has a fiduciary duty to get the best price for the trust
- All offers go through the asset manager at the servicer

### Approach: Professional, Numbers-Driven

REO conversations are fundamentally different from distressed seller conversations. No empathy play. No life-event discovery. This is a business transaction.

**Opening (call or email to listing agent):**

"Hi Becky, this is [Name] with [Company]. I'm looking at the listing on Cashel Castle Dr — MLS 55193445. I'm a cash buyer, can close in 14-21 days. I have a few questions about the property before I put together an offer. Is now a good time?"

**Key questions to ask:**
1. How long has the property been listed?
2. Have there been any offers? (If yes: were they rejected, countered, or withdrawn?)
3. Is the trust looking for a specific net number?
4. Are there any known issues with the property beyond what's visible? (Permits, liens, HOA violations?)
5. What's the best way to submit offers — your preferred format?
6. How quickly does the asset manager typically respond to offers?
7. Is there a repair addendum or AS-IS addendum required?

**After getting answers, submit offer:**

"Based on my inspection of the photos and the condition of the property — the kitchen is gutted, floors are stripped, and the roof and HVAC are 42 years old — I'm looking at $65-78K in repairs minimum. My offer is $[X] cash, 14-day close, no contingencies, AS-IS. Proof of funds attached."

### Negotiation Notes

- REO asset managers use BPOs (Broker Price Opinions) as their baseline
- Your offer needs to be justified with repair estimates and comps
- Don't lowball insultingly — they'll ignore you and move on
- If your first offer is rejected, ask: "What price range would the trust consider?" — this tells you if you're in the ballpark
- REOs often counter at 90-95% of list. Be prepared for $125-130K counter.
- Best leverage: property sitting unsold for 60+ days = more negotiating room

### What NOT to Do

- Don't ask about the previous owner's story (irrelevant, agent won't share)
- Don't use wholesaler scripts designed for distressed sellers ("what would selling solve for you?")
- Don't try creative terms — cash only with REOs
- Don't skip proof of funds — agents filter out unqualified buyers immediately
- Don't submit without a pre-approval or POF letter
